Latest Projects

Check out some of my latest projects with creative ideas.

  • Fujairah Whale and Dolphin Project

    Marine mammal observer and leader of the 1st dedicated cetaceans project in the Emirate of Fujairah which aims to Identification and long-term continuous monitoring of whales, dolphins, and other marine mammals and study of the behavior, space, and habitat used by cetaceans for resting and feeding. 

    See Project

  • Artificial Reef Project

    As the project leader, I spearheaded the development of artificial reefs using oyster shells and palm leaves to support coral rehabilitation and biodiversity conservation. The initiative involved conducting an Environmental Impact Assessment (EIA), designing the reefs, and overseeing their construction and implementation. Additionally, the project aimed to evaluate which reef design most effectively enhances fish stocks.

  • water quality monitoring

    Establish a continuous monitoring program for the reef habitat to assess the impact of human activities and identify water quality by collecting water samples and doing the lab analysis. Aiming to study how the levels of heavy metals, microbial analysis, pH, and salinity vary across different marine habitats and investigate the habitats resilient to water quality changes

  • Marine Habitat Map

    Identify and classify different marine habitats within Fujairah marine waters, such as coral reefs, seagrass beds, and sandy bottoms, and Assess the health condition of each habitat to inform conservation and management efforts. Through collaboration with the diving centers, private companies, and environmental agencies to provide us with the existing data. Moreover, using satellite imagery and doing field surveys.

  • Turtle rehabilitation

    Established a campaign to save the turtles “rescue, rehabilitate, and release back into the wild any sea turtles found sick or injured in Fujairah”, educate the community on their first response in case of an encounter with sick turtles, as well as understand the success of rehabilitation and to research turtle movements throughout the region and beyond via a satellite tracking initiative.

  • Fisheries resources assessment survey with MOCCAE & EAD

    Conducted the first Fisheries Resource Assessment Survey in Fujairah's offshore waters using the state-of-the-art vessel Jaywun, developed by EAD. Utilizing methods such as fishing traps, ROVs, sonar, acoustic sensors, and eDNA, the survey aimed to improve knowledge of fish stock diversity, distribution, abundance, and demographics. This effort supports fish conservation, sustainability, and the development of effective management strategie

  • Marine biodiversity assessment

    Evaluate the diversity and health of marine species in Fujairah waters, including turtles, fish, corals, sea snakes, and seabirds. Document their distribution and interactions, and assess threats like pollution and climate change. The goal is to provide data for effective conservation and management strategies to preserve marine biodiversity and ecosystem health

  • Genetic analysis of common bottlenose dolphin

    Project lead of the 1st genetic diversity study of common bottlenose dolphin in UAE, after establishing collaboration with Dr. Oliver from UAEU and Dr. Ada Natoli from ZU (the most expertise in this field in UAE) and learning from their knowledge.
